The THOR project

While studying at Arts et Metiers, I took part in an annual student challenge organized by the CNES. This challenge sends students in zero G to have them perform real-life microgravity experiments. I applied with my team and we won the 2018 session.

In inhabited mission, astronaut’s time cost a lot and helping them being more efficient in their mundane tasks can save a lot of money. Having a drone controlled on earth or by an Artificial Intelligence could be a life changer for these missions. But how complex is it to design a floating object in such a challenging environment ? Our challenge: Controlling a drone in microgravity with no referential axis and no previous experiences with control and drones

I successfully led this project for 1 year, from raising funds to buy parts, designing the drone, coding the controls and try to test it before the actual test day in zero G. That project was a real success for us as we were able to learn tons of new skills about flight controllers, sensors, drone and propeller design...

On the test day, we were able to follow our test procedure, adn were able to follow a straight line for 5sec, with the drone's camera targetting a printed target. Quite a big succes with a 1000 EUR budget and only 1 trial in a non-perfect micro gravity

What I did:

Leading the team (5 people)

Calculating our need (Power of the motors, cinematics)

Making tons of research to select components (battery, motors, propellers, gyroscope…)

Placing the components to center the mass

Coding the PID controller using Betaflight

Organizing the Schedule and planning

Constant debugging, prototyping, finding new ideas…
