The TOSSO - Green energy for EVs project

I joined Rossini Energy for a very short term contract. The start up was 3 years old and was specialized in designing, installing and operating Charging stations for EVs. The CEO wanted to diversify his protfolio and get a bigger product to sell, to expand the brand and get higher sales.

After exploring different potential products, we agreed on trying to build a solar carport to charge cars with Solar energy. The product had to be design, made of wood, and modular.My mission was clear: I had 4 months to design a new product and build a functional prototype in the parking lot. I had a budget of 20.000 EUR

I immediately started sketching lines on my notbook, went through some equations to estimate a decent solar surface, an annual energy production, an avergae time of charge, a rough estimation of the money benefit and ROI for a customer... After a week, I had a CAD model on Fusion 360, I was running structural simulations to resist wind and hold the pannel and already started to get in contact with potential suppliers.

The project kept going at a fast pace, I learned how to draw power electrics circuits, how to negociate prices with all of the suppliers, how to design a structure to respect the EU norms, how to select the right waterproofing solution, how to create en embedded charging station, how to remotely measure AC current with a Raspberry Pi and I enjoyed getting to know the team. After 4 month, everything was assemble and a 4-car solar carport was functional on the parking

It could charge only from the sun's energy, or from the grid and the sun for a fast charge. I had access to a real-time tracking of the current and the company's car could charge under the solar roof.

I had the huge chance to be trusted, to be mentored and supported by the CEO and the team, to find passionate people willing to share their knowledge and to work on a project that had a positive impact on the environment. That was a dream mission and that made me realize that with the proper management and a good reason to work, I would always find ways to make it happen and well.
