The Water Immersion cooling project

This ongoing experiment aims to improve data center cooling efficiency through water immersion technology. It is a personal endeavor that I lead in partnership with my company HARTING and UC Berkeley.

Data centers are responsible for about 2% of the world's energy consumption, with cooling accounting for an average of 40% of that energy. As a passionate engineer, I dream of creating better solutions for the technology that will support our future society and economy.

I believe immersion is an efficient way to cool CPUs and GPUs. Since 2024, I have been actively exploring water immersion's performance, scalability, and safety challenges. The goal is to pair it with water's incredible thermal properties—similar to direct-to-chip cooling—while avoiding electrical shorts using a special coating.

To support this effort, I put together a capstone team at UC Berkeley to help build a test bench comparing water immersion to dielectric immersion. The setup features two cooling loops connected by a heat exchanger to evaluate how quickly we can extract heat from heated PCBs and the energy efficiency of the process.

We now have a fully functional test bench and are gathering our first data sets. If you share an interest in improving data center cooling or would like to collaborate, feel free to reach out. I always love meeting like-minded people who dream of building a better world together!
