The water immersion cooling project - on going experiment to improve Data center cooling efficiency

This project is a personal endeavour, that I'm leading in partnership with my company HARTING and UC Berkeley. Data center are responsible for about 2% of the world's energy consuption, and cooling is on average 40% of the Data Center's energy need.

As a passionate engineer, I'm dreaming of a better cooling solution for the technology that's gonna be at the root of our future society and economy. I'm very convinced that immersion is a great efficient solution to cooling CPUs and GPUs. I would love to be the first to pair its benefits to the incredible thermal propreties of water that make D2C cooling solutions so efficient.

That's why, since 2024, I'm actively trying to explore the potential of water immersion cooling, and evalute its performance, its scalability and safety challenges. I've put together a capstone team at UC Berkeley to help me build a physical bench test to compare water immersion cooling to dielectric immersion cooling. The new immersion solution relies on a coating that would prevent any short circuit on the boards, while allowing for great thermal dissipation to water.

Together, we've build a fully functional test bench to test heating PCBs and understand how fast we can remove heat from them, and at what energetic cost. The test bench includes 2 cooling loops connected by a heat exchanger. Stay tuned for the first data sets the team is gathering right now...
